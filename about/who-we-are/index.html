Who We Are

The National Audit Office of Mauritius

The Director of Audit is the Head of the National Audit Office (NAO) which is an independent Constitutional body established under Section 110 of the Constitution of the Republic of Mauritius. The Director of Audit is an independent officer whose independence is protected under the Constitution.

The NAO provides independent assurance to the National Assembly on whether the government's financial transactions and statements are in order and whether public resources have been used economically, efficiently and effectively. This assurance is communicated through reports submitted to the National Assembly.

The main outputs of NAO are the Annual Report of the Director of Audit on the accounts of Ministries/Departments of Government, the Annual Financial Statements of Public Bodies, and Report on Specific Examinations. Reports are laid before the National Assembly and are available to the public.

Audit services provided by the NAO include financial, performance, compliance and IT audits for some 200 entities of the public sector including Central Government, Statutory Bodies, Local Authorities, Rodrigues Regional Assembly, Outer Islands Development Corporation, the University of Mauritius and the University of Technology.

The NAO undertakes financial audits to provide an opinion on whether the financial statements of Ministries/Departments and Public Bodies present fairly their financial position and results of operations, in accordance with applicable accounting standards and the relevant legal and regulatory requirements.

Performance audits are carried out to assess the economy, efficiency and effectiveness of major government programmes and projects. Compliance audits are conducted to ascertain adherence to laws, regulations, policies and procedures. IT audits focus on the reliability, security and efficiency of government information systems.

The NAO is a member of the International Organisation of Supreme Audit Institutions (INTOSAI), the African Organisation of English-speaking Supreme Audit Institutions (AFROSAI-E), the Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa (COMESA) and the Commission de l'Océan Indien (COI). These memberships allow the NAO to benchmark itself against international best practices and benefit from knowledge sharing and capacity building initiatives.

Constitutional Mandate

The independence and powers of the Director of Audit are enshrined in Section 110 of the Constitution of the Republic of Mauritius, ensuring that the audit function remains free from executive interference and that audit findings are reported objectively to Parliament.

International Memberships

The NAO actively participates in international and regional audit organisations, contributing to and benefiting from global audit knowledge and standards.

🌐INTOSAI

International Organisation of Supreme Audit Institutions

The worldwide organisation of government audit institutions, providing standards and best practices for external government audit.

🌍AFROSAI-E

African Organisation of English-speaking Supreme Audit Institutions

The regional body facilitating capacity building and knowledge sharing among English-speaking African Supreme Audit Institutions.

🌐COMESA

Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa

NAO participates in the COMESA SAI network, promoting regional cooperation and harmonisation of audit standards.

🏝️COI

Commission de l'Océan Indien

Active participation in the Indian Ocean Commission's audit cooperation framework for island economies.
